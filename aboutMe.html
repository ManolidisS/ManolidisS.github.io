My name is Spyridon Manolidis, and I'm otherwise known as Spyros (or ManolidisS online).
I was originally born in Greece, but now am proud to also call Wales my home.
I am currently 14 (almost 15), and have a really strong passion for mathematics, physics, and computer science.
I also play and really enjoy the violin!
As of writing this, I am almost halfway through my first GCSE year (Year 10). Things have certainly gotten more difficult, but I must admit I enjoy the challenge!
I aspire to one day change the world through my passions, primarily physics and maths, and I am on the hunt for altruistic opportunities which will allow me to develop and mature as a scientist.


By the time I finish year 11, I intend to have 13 GCSEs:

  • English Language
  • English Literature
  • Physics
  • Chemistry
  • Biology
  • Welsh

I also try to involve myself in many extra-curriculars to explore and develop my different passions and hobbies. For example, in school I attend:

  • The school orchestra
  • The school choir
  • My year group's vocal ensemble, Diatonics.

These commitments are crucial for the development of my social skills and teach me valuable lessons, such as the importance of teamwork and coordination.
I also try and keep myself busy outside of school by:

  • Attending tutoring lessons in Physics/Maths which drastically helps to improve my intuition and introduces me to new, exciting problems.
  • Taking violin (and music theory) lessons at a grade 8 and grade 5 standard, respectively. This is not only one of my passions, but it links well with topics in physics and maths such as standing waves.
  • Having lessons in Greek to maintain my proficiency in my mother tounge.
  • Having lessons in German to learn a new language and open new opportunities.
  • Taking karate lessons in my local karate club which teaches me discipline, focus, and the importance of practice!
  • Attending CCVG's high schools' orchestra which makes me appreciate the incredible things we can do as a community, with each instrument playing their part!
  • Having Byzantine Music lessons, which make me value my heritage and culture.
  • Doing horseriding at a local school. This allows me to connect with nature and appreciate the beauty of the world we live in.
